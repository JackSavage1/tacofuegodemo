35 Locations 22% Revenue Leak Southeast US Pre-Sales Discovery

Taco Fuego is a fast-casual taco brand with 35 locations across the Southeast. They've grown 3x in four years on the strength of loyal regulars and a cult following on social media — but their digital ordering infrastructure hasn't kept pace. Right now, more than a third of their digital orders flow through DoorDash and Grubhub, and the commission fees are quietly bleeding the business.

This brief documents the discovery session, product mapping, and projected ROI for a potential Olo engagement — prepared by Jack Savage, Solutions Consultant.

Commission bleed on 3rd-party platforms

DoorDash and Grubhub charge 15–30% per order. On $4.2M GMV, that's up to $924K/year leaving the business — while Taco Fuego owns none of those guest relationships.

Discovery Call

The questions I'd ask
in the first 30 minutes

Good discovery isn't interrogation — it's guided curiosity. Each question below is designed to surface a specific business problem that maps directly to an Olo capability. The goal: let the brand tell me where it hurts, then show them exactly where the solution lives.

01
"What percentage of your digital orders today come from your own website or app vs. DoorDash and Grubhub?"
Intent: Quantify the 3rd-party dependency and commission exposure.
02
"When a guest orders on DoorDash — what data do you actually receive about that order?"
Intent: Surface the guest data blindspot and build urgency around ownership.
03
"How many tablets or devices does your kitchen staff manage during a typical lunch rush?"
Intent: Expose operational friction; set up the Dispatch / Rails unified inbox story.
04
"Do you have any way today to know which guests are your top 10% by spend — and reach them directly?"
Intent: Make the loyalty/guest data gap concrete and personal.
05
"What does your current online ordering experience look like on mobile — and who built it?"
Intent: Assess tech debt and open the door to Olo's white-label ordering frontend.
06
"If you could fix one thing about your payment processing today, what would it be?"
Intent: Open-ended; often surfaces fraud, chargebacks, or reconciliation pain — all Olo Pay wins.
07
"Are you managing menu updates across all 35 locations manually, or do you have a central system?"
Intent: Uncover menu management pain — a major Olo Order differentiator at scale.
08
"What does success look like for you 12 months from now — what number moves that tells your board the digital program is working?"
Intent: Anchor the conversation to outcomes, not features. Maps directly to Olo's ROI story.
Olo Platform

Three products.
One connected system.

Olo isn't a single product — it's three interconnected suites that compound on each other. Here's what each one does, explained the way I'd explain it to a restaurant ops manager who doesn't care about APIs.

🧾

Olo Order

Your digital front door — owned by you, not a marketplace.

Olo Order is the platform that powers your restaurant's own online ordering — your website, your app, your kiosk. When a guest orders directly from you, you keep the margin, you own the data, and you control the experience.

Plain English: Think of it like the difference between selling a concert ticket on your own website vs. selling it through Ticketmaster. Ticketmaster charges you a fee and owns the customer relationship. Olo Order is your own ticketing system — zero commission, your guest data.
  • Single menu management hub — update prices, items, or hours across all 35 locations in one place
  • White-label ordering on your website and app — looks and feels like your brand, not Olo's
  • Dispatch: manages delivery through DoorDash Drive, Uber Eats, and others — without paying marketplace commission
  • Rails: one integration that routes 3rd-party orders directly into your POS — no more tablet chaos
  • Catering module for high-ticket group orders with custom lead times and workflows
💳

Olo Pay

A restaurant-specific payment processor that protects revenue and simplifies operations.

Olo Pay replaces your generic payment processor with one built specifically for restaurants. It handles both online and in-store transactions in one dashboard, uses machine learning to reduce fraud, and typically increases the percentage of legitimate transactions that go through successfully.

Plain English: Imagine your current payment processor is a security guard who waves everyone through but lets fraud slip by too. Olo Pay is a smarter guard — it uses AI trained on millions of restaurant transactions to let the good ones through faster and stop fraud before it hits your bank account.
  • Typically lifts authorization rates by ~5% — meaning more legitimate orders actually complete
  • Machine-learning fraud detection reduces chargebacks and dispute costs
  • Single dashboard for online and in-store payments — one reconciliation, not four
  • Digital wallet support (Apple Pay, Google Pay) for faster checkout and lower cart abandonment
  • Card-present (kiosk/POS) and card-not-present (online) unified on one platform
📡

Olo Engage

Turn guest transaction data into repeat revenue through personalized marketing.

Engage is Olo's guest data and marketing platform. It collects every order, every interaction, and every payment into a single guest profile — then helps you act on it. Segment your guests, run targeted campaigns, predict who's about to churn, and bring them back before they go.

Plain English: Right now, Taco Fuego's most loyal guest — the person who orders every Tuesday — is invisible to you digitally. Engage makes them visible. You can see their lifetime value, send them a personalized offer on Monday night, and measure whether they came back Tuesday. It's the difference between guessing and knowing.
  • Guest Data Platform (GDP): unified guest profiles built from every order across every channel
  • Predictive guest lifetime value (GLV) and churn risk scoring — powered by machine learning
  • Automated email and SMS campaigns with AI-generated copy (OpenAI-powered)
  • Sentiment analysis — automatically read and respond to guest reviews across platforms
  • Loyalty program integration — connect your rewards program to the full guest data picture
